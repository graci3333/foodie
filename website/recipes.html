Adobo

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 to 2 lbs chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks work well)
  • 1 onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 1 head garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (cane or white vinegar)
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns (crushed)
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:

    2. In a bowl, combine chicken, soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns. Marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to absorb.

  2. Saut√© Onion:

    3. Heat oil in a wide, heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add sliced onions and cook until they become soft and translucent.

  3. Cook the Chicken:

    4. Add the marinated chicken to the pan. Cook until the chicken is browned on all sides.

  4. Add Vinegar and Simmer:

    5. Pour in the vinegar and allow it to boil for a minute to cook off the strong vinegar taste. Reduce heat and let it simmer for another 2 minutes.

  5. Add Soy Sauce, Bay Leaves, and Water:

    6. Pour in the soy sauce and add the bay leaves. Add water and bring to a boil.

  6. Simmer Until Cooked:

    7. Lower the heat, cover the pan, and simmer for about 30 to 40 minutes or until the chicken is tender and the sauce is reduced. Stir occasionally.

  7. Adjust Seasoning:

    8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. You can add a bit of sugar to balance the flavors if desired.

  8. Serve:

    9. Serve hot over steamed rice. Garnish with chopped green onions if desired.

Enjoy your homemade Adobo! Feel free to modify the recipe based on your preferences and ingredient availability.



Sinigang

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 to 2 lbs pork ribs or pork belly, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 large onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 radish, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bundle string beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 2 pieces green chili peppers (siling pansigang), optional
  • 1 bundle water spinach (kangkong), separated into stalks and leaves
  • 1 packet (about 40g) sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • 1 liter water
  • Fish sauce (patis) or salt to taste
  • Pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the Pork:

    2. In a large pot, place the pork ribs or belly and cover with water. Bring to a boil and remove any scum that rises to the surface. Simmer until the pork is tender.

  2. Add Vegetables:

    3. Add onions and tomatoes to the pot. Simmer until the tomatoes are soft and the flavors are infused into the broth.

  3. Add Sinigang Mix:

    4. Pour in the sinigang mix and stir to dissolve. Adjust the level of sourness according to your preference.

  4. Add Vegetables (Continued):

    5. Add radish and eggplant. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.

  5. Add String Beans and Water Spinach:

    6. Add string beans and the stalks of water spinach. Cook until both are tender.

  6. Adjust Seasoning:

    7. Season with fish sauce or salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the sourness if needed by adding more sinigang mix.

  7. Add Chili Peppers (Optional):

    8. If you like it spicy, add green chili peppers to the pot.

  8. Add Water Spinach Leaves:

    9. Just before serving, add the water spinach leaves. They cook quickly and maintain their vibrant green color.

  9. Serve:

    10. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy the comforting and flavorful Pork Sinigang!

Feel free to customize the recipe by adding other vegetables or using different protein sources. Enjoy your Sinigang!
